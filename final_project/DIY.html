Historical Investigation: Do it Yourself

1. Pick an asylum

You may choose one of the asylums featured in the exhibits. You can also research a different one. Like me you might investigate an institution features in a movie or television show. You could also search Flickr which has a category of asylum pictures submitted by enthusiasts who explore abandoned urban places.

2. Research your primary sources

Again using the primary sources that I use in the exhibits is fine. You could also do your own research. Google Books Advanced Search (click Full View Only for the best results) has full text documents that relate to many institutions. Archive.org and HathiTrust Digital Library are other excellent places to begin your research.

3. Share something that you find interesting

Search the document for references to the institution until you come across something that interests you. If you have chosen to search the asylums and documents that I have featured, here is where you find a clip that interests you personally. Take a screen shot of the image or quote it making sure that you tell your readers where you found it. Then, just as I did, speculate on what you think it means and justify why you think it might be important or interesting.

4. Publish your findings somewhere on the web

If you can make your own site, that is great, but publishing on your class blog would work just as well. You could also use social media like Tumblr. I would also like to display your findings on this site so please send me the link.
